The Broad Museum - Los Angeles

The Broad Museum is a post-war and contemporary art museum that was constructed at Grand Avenue in downtown Los Angeles, California, US, by philanthropists Eli and Edythe Broad. The building provides 50,000ft² of gallery space and 21,000ft² of climate-controlled archive storage space. The project further incorporates an outdoor public plaza, whose construction was completed in 2014, an outdoor restaurant and underground parking area to be owned and operated by the community redevelopment agency (CRA).
